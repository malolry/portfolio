Project Overview

As I enjoy learning new concepts, I often challenge myself with new projects. In this case, I took on the role of a Data Science consultant for a Parisian hotel. My objective was to develop and implement a protocol to address the hotel's hypothetical challenge: improving its rating on Booking.com.

First of all, I developed a Python algorithm using Selenium library to scrape the entire hotel's reviews from Booking.com (find the script on my GitHub). I obtained a dataset composed of 6 features: title, country, score, positive part, negative part, and date. I prefered to keep the reviews anonymous to avoid biais and unuseful information.

Then, I used Natural Language Processing (NLP) to keep only the relevant information from the reviews so that I can analyze them and provide insights :

  • Formatting of the date and score columns
  • Translating the "Negative part" column into English (the only column used to derive insights, as we are focusing on identifying issues in the hotel)
  • Removing stop words, performing lemmatization, and tokenizing words


Now that the data is clean, I compiled a list of the most common words in the negative reviews: "room," "small," "bathroom," and "little." These terms suggest that the hotel's infrastructure is perceived as too cramped. However, I cannot advise the hotel manager to enlarge the rooms by removing walls. Fortunately, there are numerous architectural techniques that can create the illusion of larger spaces, particularly through the use of color schemes and the strategic selection of wall coverings.

I particularly enjoyed working on this project as it allowed me to apply my skills in data science and natural language processing. There is still much to explore, and the project could be further developed in various ways to extract even more relevant and useful insights for the hotel. It could also be scaled to other hotels or even other industries.
